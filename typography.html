Typographic Systems

Axial System: Text is arranged along a single axis, creating a clean and organized layout with a sense of direction.

Radial System: Text radiates outward from a central point, creating dynamic and energetic compositions.

Dilatational System: Text expands in concentric circles, conveying a sense of motion and rhythm.

Random System: Text is arranged without a clear structure, often resulting in playful and experimental layouts.

Grid System: Text is aligned to a structured grid, creating a balanced and harmonious design.

Modular System: Text is organized into consistent blocks or modules, emphasizing uniformity and repetition.

Transitional System: Text flows smoothly between different arrangements, creating a sense of movement and connection.

Bilateral System: Text is mirrored on either side of an axis, resulting in a balanced and symmetrical layout.
